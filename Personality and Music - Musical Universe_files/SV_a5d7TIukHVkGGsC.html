| Personality

The Big Five is the most widely accepted model today for understanding personality.

This model organizes peoples personality into five explicit dimensions commonly referred to as: Openness to Experience (O), Conscientiousness (C), Extraversion (E), Agreeableness (A), and Emotional Stability (ES). 



 

These traits are not a classification or psychopathology, but are rather a description of characteristics that are present to some degree in all people. These traits emerge across cultures around the world and are caused by genetic and environmental factors1.

Your Openness score: Extremely Low (0.1 to 2nd Percentile)
Those high on Openness have an appreciation for aesthetics and the arts. They tend to be liberal, have active imaginations, and are inner-directed. The opposite of Openness is closed-mindedness.

Your Conscientiousness score: Extremely Low (0.1 to 2nd Percentile)
Those high on Conscientiousness are self-disciplined, strive for achievement, and prefer order. They like to have things planned in advance rather than to do things spontaneously.
 
Your Extraversion score: Very Low (3 to 8th Percentile)
Those high on Extraversion are talkative, sociable, and assertive. The opposite end of Extraversion is Introversion. Whereas Extraverts get energy from being with other people, Introverts get energy from being alone.
 
Your Agreeableness score: High (76 to 91st Percentile)
Those high on Agreeableness get along with others. They are be modest, cooperative, and tender minded. However, because of their avoidance of conflict they can find leadership positions challenging and difficult.
 
Your Emotional Stability score: Average (25 to 75th Percentile)
Those high on Emotional Stability are self-assured, confident, and do not have difficulty with self-esteem. The opposite pole of Emotional Stability is Neuroticism. Those high on Neuroticism tend to be easily upset, angry, and anxious.

| Musical Preferences

The average Westerner is exposed to music for a minimum of 16% of their waking lives10.

The most recent advance in music preference research has shown that we can organize our musical preferences into five distinct dimensions: Mellow, Unpretentious, Sophisticated, Intense, and Contemporary musicconveniently named the MUSIC preference model11.



Your preference for Mellow music: Average (25 to 75th Percentile)
Mellow music is defined as romantic, relaxing, sad, slow, and quiet; often heard in genres of soft rock, R & B, and adult contemporary.

Your preference for Unpretentious music: Low (9 to 24th percentile)
Unpretentious music is defined as uncomplicated, relaxing, unaggressive, soft, and acoustic, and primarily from the country, folk, and singer/songwriter music genres.

Your preference for Sophisticated music: High (76 to 91st Percentile)
Sophisticated music is defined as inspiring, intelligent, complex, and dynamic, and were from the classical, operatic, avant-garde, world beat, and traditional jazz music genres.

Your preference for Intense music:  Average (25 to 75th Percentile)
Intense music is defined as distorted, loud, aggressive, and not relaxing, romantic, nor inspiring, and were from the classic rock, punk, heavy metal, and power pop music genres.

Your preference for Contemporary music: Average (25 to 75th Percentile)
Contemporary usic is defined as percussive, electric, and not sad, and from the rap, electronica, Latin, acid jazz, and Euro pop music genres.

Music Facts.
  • When people listen to their favorite songs, brain regions responsible for communication and consolidation become activated12.
  • Musical preferences are driven by both perceived acoustic features and social connotations from to the music11.
  • Musical preferences for dissonant and consonant music are influenced in part by culture13.
 
  • People form stereotypes based solely on a persons musical preferences, and these stereotypes contain a kernel of truth14.
  • On average, music preferences are the first topic of conversation amongst strangers14.   
  • Preferences based on the MUSIC model can be as accurate at predicting a listeners personality as a coworker3.
  • The MUSIC model is replicated within genres. That is, peoples preferences for a specific genre can be organized into the MUSIC dimensions. For example, preferences for rock music can be broken down into preferences for Mellow rock, Unpretentious rock, Sophisticated rock, Intense rock, and Contemporary rock15.
  • Preferences for Intense music peaks in adolescence coinciding with rebellious stages and identity formation in childhood16.

Score Calculation.

The excerpts were selected with a systematic method with the aim of representing the entire sonic space in Western music16. The excerpts represent a total of 16 genres and subgenres. To limit confounding variables, the excerpts are from songs that are likely to be new to the listener. Your preferences were then entered into an algorithm that has been established in previous research to determine scores outlined by the MUSIC model2.
